MODULE 1 | ABOUT THE INTERNSHIP
================================================================================

--- 1.1 | What is the Vicharanashala internship? ---
A two-month internship run by Vicharanashala, a research lab at IIT Ropar. You
will work on a real open-source project under a mentor, after a short training
phase tailored to where you already are. The internship is free — we do not
charge, and the work is real.

--- 1.2 | What is VINS? ---
VINS is the Vicharanashala Internship — an online programme open to anyone who
clears our interview. The work is real open-source contribution under a mentor,
the certificate is from the Vicharanashala Lab for Education Design at IIT
Ropar, and the programme itself is free (we charge nothing). There is no
stipend.

If you are seeing a yellow VINS panel on your result page, you are selected.

--- 1.3 | What are the phases of VINS, and what do the badges mean? ---
VINS is structured as four phases. Each one is marked by a badge.

  BRONZE (Phase 1): A short training period at the start, planned around what
  you already know. If you arrive already comfortable with the basics, your
  mentor may skip Bronze and put you straight on to the project.

  SILVER (Phase 2): The main work. You contribute to a real open-source project
  under a Vicharanashala mentor. Finishing Bronze and Silver completes your
  internship and earns the certificate.

  GOLD (Phase 3): A recognition awarded during Silver if your contribution
  stands on its own as a meaningful feature, not just a small fix.

  PLATINUM (Phase 4): A standing invitation to visit the lab any time during
  the year after your internship ends. We help with travel through a small
  visit stipend.

Most interns finish at Bronze + Silver — that is exactly what the certificate
is for. Gold and Platinum are extras you can pick up if your work makes the
case for them.

--- 1.4 | Who is the internship for? Are alumni eligible? ---
The internship is for currently-enrolled students at any college or university
— undergraduate, postgraduate, or doctoral. The NOC requirement reflects this:
we ask for institutional consent that you can commit your time to this
internship.

Candidates who have already graduated and are not currently enrolled in any
programme are NOT eligible for this cycle. If you re-enrol later (higher
studies, etc.), you are welcome to apply again in a future cycle.

--- 1.5 | Is this the same as IIT Ropar's official Summer Research Internship? ---
No. Summership 2026 is a VLED Lab initiative. The certificate is issued by the
Vicharanashala Lab for Education Design, NOT centrally by the institute. IIT
Ropar runs a separate institutional summer research internship through its own
office. Do not represent Summership 2026 as equivalent to that programme.

--- 1.6 | I have classes/exams during the internship period. Can I take leave? ---
Leave is NOT permitted. If you are also attending classes or exams, you will be
relieved from the internship immediately and will need to join the next batch
when it starts.

MODULE 2 | TIMING AND DATES
================================================================================

--- 2.1 | When can I start? ---
You can start any time in 2026 — VINS is flexible on the start date — but two
rules apply:

  HARD RULE: Your internship must finish by 31 December 2026. That date is
  non-negotiable. Whatever start you pick, your end date (start + 2 months,
  with up to 1 month grace) must land on or before 31 December 2026.

  STRONG RECOMMENDATION: Start as soon as possible. The earlier you join, the
  more of the May–July main cohort you catch. Three things make starting earlier
  materially better:
    - Cohort networking: peer discussions and connections happen during this
      window; later starters are largely solo.
    - TA support is concentrated in May–July; after this, TAs return to their
      own college work and bandwidth is thinner.
    - Training rolls out with the cohort, not piecemeal.

If starting now is genuinely impossible (exams, unavoidable commitments), you
can begin later and still complete — but the cohort effect and support will be
lighter, and the December cap means a late start leaves no room for slippage.

--- 2.2 | How long is the internship? ---
Two months from your chosen start date, with an optional one-month grace
period if you need it. End must land on or before 31 December 2026.

--- 2.3 | Can I start in July, August, or later if I have exams now? ---
Yes — but only if your exams genuinely make an earlier start impossible. Wait
until your exams are done, then opt in and start. Do not attempt to juggle this
internship with ongoing exams. Make sure your chosen start date plus 2 months
(or 3 with grace) lands on or before 31 December 2026.

--- 2.4 | Can I start with the cohort and take a break during my exam window? ---
No. This arrangement is not offered.

VINS is a full-attention internship — six to ten hours a day, sometimes more.
Splitting that with college exams damages both sides: the project loses momentum,
the exams suffer, and the mentor invests in someone who can only half-engage.

If your exams fall inside the cohort duration, defer your start to after your
exams end, opt in then, and run the internship at full attention.

WARNING: If we later learn that a candidate was sitting college exams during
their internship period, we reserve the right to terminate the internship or
withhold the certificate — including after the internship has otherwise been
completed.

--- 2.5 | Can I get an exemption for an exam scheduled during the internship? ---
No. The 55-day continuous window is non-negotiable. We cannot offer an
exemption for an exam during this period. Split attention genuinely damages
both your exam preparation and your internship work.

--- 2.6 | Are orientation session recordings shared with interns? ---
Recordings of the sessions will NOT be provided. We may provide access to an
abridged version of a talk or session if we consider it important, but this is
not guaranteed for every session. Project or group assignments cannot be changed
after watching them.

MODULE 3 | NOC (NO OBJECTION CERTIFICATE)
================================================================================

--- 3.1 | What dates do I put on the NOC? ---
Default: your chosen start date → your start + 2 months (with up to 1 month
grace), ensuring the end date is on or before 31 December 2026. Pick the
earliest start date you can realistically make — the May–July summer window is
the main cohort.

If the NOC will be signed on a specific later date, pick a start date after
the signature date.

--- 3.2 | Who can sign the NOC? ---
Any authorised signatory at your college: HOD, Acting HOD (during holidays),
Principal, Dean, Director, or Training & Placement Officer.

For dual-degree students: either institution can sign — pick whichever is easier.
For IITM BS Online Degree (standalone) students: any officer from the BS office
can sign.

--- 3.3 | When do I submit the NOC? Is the deadline hard? ---
The deadline is NOT hard — there is no specific cut-off date. But for the
internship to begin properly, submit it as early as possible and join the
current summer cohort.

IMPORTANT: Late submission means you miss the cohort window, your mentor may
already be busy, and you lose the cohort + TA support that make this internship
genuinely good. Submit early.

--- 3.4 | What format should I use? Do I need to design the NOC myself? ---
No — we provide a printable NOC format. Steps:
  1. Log in to samagama.in after your result is out.
  2. Click "Download blank NOC" on your dashboard.
  3. Take a printout and get it physically signed and stamped.
  4. Scan it and upload the signed PDF using "Upload signed NOC" on the dashboard.

You do not need to draft anything yourself, and you do not need college
letterhead — the format we provide is the canonical layout.

--- 3.5 | What if my college gives me an NOC in their own format? ---
A college's own NOC format is acceptable, as long as all four required entries
are present:
  1. The signing authority's HANDWRITTEN SIGNATURE (most important item).
  2. The signing authority's OFFICIAL EMAIL ADDRESS (we cross-check to verify).
  3. Your FULL NAME.
  4. YOUR SIGNATURE.

If your college's format does not include a place for your signature, sign
clearly and prominently anywhere on the document before uploading.

--- 3.6 | Does the NOC need to be signed by hand? ---
Yes. Three things are required on the NOC:
  1. The authorised signatory's HANDWRITTEN SIGNATURE.
  2. The institutional RUBBER STAMP / SEAL in the designated area.
  3. The signatory's EMAIL ADDRESS filled in the designated field (we
     automatically cross-check with that person to verify the signature).

Digital signatures are NOT accepted on the PDF path. If a physically-signed
printout is impractical for your HOD, use the email-forward path in section 3.7
— it is fully equivalent.

--- 3.7 | Can my HOD email the NOC instead of signing a printout? ---
Yes — there is a fully-equivalent email-forward path. Use whichever is easier;
you do not need to do both.

HOW IT WORKS:
  1. Download the text NOC from your dashboard
     ("Download text NOC (email path)" button).
  2. Fill in the student-side fields, then email the file to your HOD and ask
     them to forward it.
  3. Your HOD forwards the email to sudarshan@iitrpr.ac.in from their OFFICIAL
     INSTITUTIONAL EMAIL ADDRESS, with the subject line:
     "NOC for my student <Your Full Name>"

The forward itself counts as the signature — the HOD's official institutional
email address is the verification.

TWO NON-NEGOTIABLE CONDITIONS:
  - The forward must come from the HOD's OFFICIAL INSTITUTIONAL EMAIL ADDRESS
    (a college/university domain) — NOT a personal Gmail or Outlook account.
  - The subject line MUST start with "NOC for my student" or it may be missed.

After the forward arrives, confirmation and offer-letter issuance follow the
normal timeline (about 24 to 48 hours). Candidates who use this path do NOT
also need to upload a signed PDF.

--- 3.8 | How do I download and upload the NOC? ---
Both happen on your dashboard at samagama.in once your result is out. You will
see a NOC section with two buttons:

  - "Download blank NOC" — saves the printable NOC format PDF.
  - "Upload signed NOC (PDF)" — opens a file picker; file must be a PDF of
    at most 1 MB.

The NOC section appears in three places on the dashboard (all backed by the
same endpoints — use whichever is convenient):
  - A compact pill in the dark header bar at the top of every screen.
  - A standalone NOC card on the dashboard, between the Results card and the
    Talk-to-Yaksha button.
  - A NOC section at the bottom of your full Result message.

The chat surface no longer carries any NOC affordance — please use the
dashboard buttons. If you cannot see the buttons, make sure you are logged in
as the email that received the result, and that your result has been released.

--- 3.9 | What if my NOC is not formally verified yet? ---
NOC verification takes time — typically anywhere between an hour and one full
working day from the moment you upload.

If you need your offer letter sooner, use the TENTATIVE ROUTE:
  - Upload a self-declaration on your profile.
  - A tentative offer letter will be issued to you immediately.
  - The formal offer letter follows once your NOC clears verification.

--- 3.10 | My online course (Masai, NPTEL, Coursera, etc.) won't issue an NOC. ---
The internship is open ONLY to candidates currently enrolled in a full-time
degree programme at a recognised college or university. Online-only courses —
Masai Institute, NPTEL/MOOC, Coursera, Udacity, bootcamps — do NOT by
themselves make a candidate eligible.

  - If you are CONCURRENTLY ENROLLED in a full-time degree programme: obtain an
    NOC from that college and upload it via the dashboard.
  - If your ONLY academic engagement is the online course: the internship is not
    open to you in this cycle. Apply again in a future cycle once enrolled in a
    full-time programme.

--- 3.11 | My HOD wants written confirmation before signing my NOC. What do I show them? ---
Your selection is confirmed the moment your yellow VINS result panel appears on
samagama.in — that is the official confirmation. There is no separate written
letter issued before the NOC step by default.

If your HOD insists on a document in hand, use the TENTATIVE OFFER LETTER ROUTE:
  1. Log in to samagama.in and open your profile.
  2. Upload a brief self-declaration (short statement that you intend to start
     and will submit your signed NOC once approved).
  3. A tentative offer letter on Vicharanashala letterhead is issued immediately.
  4. Hand the tentative offer letter to your HOD — it serves as the written
     confirmation they need to sign your NOC.
  5. Once your HOD signs the NOC and you upload it, the formal offer letter is
     issued automatically (see sections 4.3 and 3.9).

--- 3.12 | Can Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar or an IIT Ropar faculty member sign my NOC? ---
No. Your NOC must be signed by an authorised signatory at the institution where
YOU are enrolled — HOD, Dean, Principal, or T&P Officer. Prof. Sudarshan
Iyengar is a faculty member at IIT Ropar and cannot sign your NOC in a personal
capacity.

REGARDING ELIGIBILITY: The IIT Ropar/Masai online AIML programme is an
online-only certification course. Even if offered jointly with an IIT, it does
NOT meet the full-time degree enrolment requirement on its own.

  - If you are concurrently enrolled in a full-time degree elsewhere: obtain the
    NOC from that institution.
  - If your only enrolment is the IIT Ropar/Masai online programme: you are NOT
    eligible for this internship cycle.

--- 4.1 | How do I know I am selected? ---
If you can see your YELLOW VINS result panel on samagama.in, you are selected.
There is no separate selection step or confirmation email.

--- 4.2 | How do I opt into VINS? ---
Tell Yaksha in the chat: "I want to take up the online internship without
stipend." Yaksha will confirm. Opting in is the selection — no separate
confirmation email is sent at that stage.

--- 4.3 | When do I get the offer letter? ---
There are two paths:

  PATH 1 — FORMAL OFFER LETTER (default). Issued once BOTH of these are done:
    1. Your signed NOC has been verified (typically 1 hour to 1 working day
       after upload).
    2. You have confirmed your internship start and end dates on the dashboard
       (see section 4.5).
  Once both are done, the formal offer letter is issued automatically — usually
  within an hour of the second step completing.

  PATH 2 — TENTATIVE OFFER LETTER (faster). Upload a self-declaration on your
  profile instead. A tentative offer letter is issued immediately, and the
  formal letter follows once your NOC clears verification.

Either letter is on Vicharanashala letterhead and serves as your official
document for college/employer records.

The offer letter lives on your dashboard at samagama.in, NOT in your email.
When issued, you will receive a notification email from no-reply@vicharanashala.ai
with the subject: "Your Vicharanashala Offer Letter is ready — please log in to
download." The actual letter is downloaded from the Offer Letter card on your
dashboard. If you do not see the notification email, check spam/promotions — but
always log in to samagama.in and click "Download Offer Letter" as the primary path.

--- 4.4 | Will I get a certificate? ---
Yes — every intern who completes the internship gets a certificate from
Vicharanashala, IIT Ropar. The internship is genuinely demanding; candidates
who drop out mid-way do NOT get a certificate.

--- 4.5 | How do I confirm my internship dates? ---
After opting into VINS via Yaksha (see section 4.2):
  1. Log in to samagama.in.
  2. Find the yellow card titled "Confirm your internship dates" on the dashboard.
  3. The two date pickers pre-fill with sensible defaults for the current cohort.
  4. If those work for you, hit "Save dates". Otherwise edit them.
  5. A green confirmation appears once saved.

Your end date must be on or before 31 December 2026. You can edit any time from
the same card. Dates must match what your HOD signed off on in your NOC.

--- 4.6 | I am a Minor/Major in AI student from IIT Ropar. Do I need an NOC? ---
The Minor/Major in AI course from IIT Ropar is a certification course — there
will be a DIFFERENT TRACK of internship equivalent for these students. Write
to us separately. To be part of THIS internship programme you must be a
registered student in a UG/PG programme with a university. This internship is
exclusively for students, not working professionals.

--- 4.7 | How do I accept the offer letter? ---
Once your formal offer letter arrives, use this EXACT FORM — it is the first
attention-to-detail check of the internship:

  1. Reply All on the offer-letter email thread (sudarshan@iitrpr.ac.in is
     already CC'd — keep that address on your reply).

  2. In the body, paste the following acceptance statement EXACTLY as printed,
     with your full name inserted and a date added:

     > I, [Full Name], confirm that I have read, understood, and accepted all
     > terms, conditions, and obligations set out in this offer letter and in
     > the program FAQ at samagama.in. I formally accept the offer of Summer
     > Internship 2026.

  RULES:
    - Copy-paste this sentence as-is.
    - Do NOT paraphrase, shorten, or rearrange the words.
    - The reply must reach us within 5 DAYS of the offer letter being sent.
    - Do not change the recipient fields.

  ALTERNATIVE: Download the offer letter PDF, fill in your name and date in the
  acceptance block, sign and scan as a PDF, and attach to your reply.

--- 4.8 | What if I reply without using the exact acceptance format? ---
The offer is WITHDRAWN, effective immediately, with no further correspondence.
The withdrawal is final.

What counts as NON-COMPLIANT:
  - Paraphrasing ("I happily accept", "I gladly confirm", etc.)
  - Sending only "I accept" or "Yes, accepted"
  - Missing the date
  - Missing the FAQ-reference clause
  - An attached photo or scan of an unfilled or undated offer letter

What does NOT count as non-compliant (minor leniency):
  - Single-word slips ("the offer letter" vs. "this offer letter")
  - Obvious typing mistakes in an otherwise complete attestation

--- 4.9 | I received a withdrawal email because I didn't accept correctly. Can it be reversed? ---
There is an appeal path, with conditions. The withdrawal stands by default.

  DO NOT reply to the withdrawal email itself — replies are not read.

  TO APPEAL: Send a fresh email to: sudarshansudarshan@gmail.com

  The subject line MUST be exactly:
  "Request to Reconsider: Confirmation Reply Error"

  Copy-paste this subject line as-is. Any typo, extra word, missing colon, or
  change in capitalisation/punctuation will cause the appeal to be missed.

  In the body: state an apology and the reason. If genuine, we will respond
  within 24 hours.

  NOTE: A granted appeal does NOT restore the offer on the standard track — it
  places you on a separate track with an additional assignment (a short course
  on attention to detail) that you must complete before the internship proceeds.

--- 4.10 | I sent my acceptance but my dashboard doesn't update. Is that normal? ---
Yes. The dashboard tracks NOC, internship dates, and offer letter — it does NOT
track the acceptance email. After sending your acceptance reply, you will not
see a new green tick or status change. This is normal and expected.

Acceptance emails are processed manually. If your reply was compliant, no
further action is needed — you are accepted and the internship will proceed.

If several working days pass with no response, log in to samagama.in and type
#escalate in the Yaksha chat.

--- 4.11 | How do I change my internship dates before the offer letter is issued? ---
Until your formal offer letter has been generated, you can change your start
and end dates yourself from the dashboard at any time — open the "Confirm
Internship Dates" card under "My Journey" and edit the dates. End date must
still be on or before 31 December 2026.

This self-edit path is ONLY available until the offer letter is generated. Once
the offer letter has been sent, the dates are locked — see section 4.16 to
change them after the offer letter is issued.

--- 4.12 | When and how do I get the Zoom link for the kickoff meeting? ---
The kickoff orientation is held for the MAIN SUMMER COHORT ONLY (candidates
starting at the opening of the May–July window). The Zoom link is delivered via:
  - Email to your registered samagama.in address.
  - Your Yaksha chat portal on samagama.in.

If your start date is later, there is no separate kickoff event for you.

If you cannot register with the Zoom link or have not received it, type
#escalate in the Yaksha chat on samagama.in.

--- 4.13 | My start date is approaching and I don't have my NOC or selection letter yet. ---
Do not wait for the selection confirmation letter. Use the self-declaration form
to obtain your provisional offer letter. You have a 15-DAY WINDOW to submit the
proper NOC and get an actual offer letter. Failure to submit the NOC within this

--- 4.14 | When does my internship actually begin? Will I receive a notification? ---
Your internship begins on the start date you confirmed on the dashboard — the
same date printed on your offer letter. There is NO separate "your internship
has begun" email, chat message, or dashboard notification on the day itself.

On the morning of your start date, log in to samagama.in. Yaksha will guide you
through the Day-1 steps of the Bronze phase.

If your dashboard appears unchanged, do a hard refresh and re-login. If it still
looks the same, type #escalate in the chat.

--- 4.15 | Can I switch from VINS (online) to VISE (offline) after being selected? ---
No. The two tracks are finalised at the interview stage. We do not move
candidates between them. VISE has a fixed on-campus capacity planned around
mentor bandwidth, hostel availability, and stipend allocation.

VINS is NOT a consolation track. The project, mentor, and certificate are the
same as VISE — what differs is the mode (online) and the absence of a
fellowship. Confirm your VINS start dates and get your NOC uploaded — you are

--- 4.16 | Can I change my internship dates after the offer letter has been issued? ---
Yes — but the dashboard self-edit path (section 4.11) is no longer available.
Once the offer letter is generated, the Confirm Internship Dates card is locked.

HOW TO REQUEST THE CHANGE:
The official from your institution who signed your NOC must send an email:
  - To: harshdeep.r@vicharanashala.ai
  - CC: sudarshan@iitrpr.ac.in
  - Subject: "Date change request — <Your Full Name> — Vicharanashala Summership 2026"
  - Body: the reason for the date change, plus the new proposed start and end dates.
  - Attachment: a justification letter written by you (the student), signed by
    your NOC approver to endorse the reasoning.

The email must originate from the NOC approver's own institutional address — a
forward by the student is NOT accepted.

Once we receive that email, we will unlock the Confirm Internship Dates card on
your dashboard so you can re-save the new dates, and we will re-issue the
formal offer letter. The earlier letter ceases to be valid the moment the new
one is issued.

--- 4.17 | How do I get the link for daily Zoom standups? Are they mandatory? ---
Daily Zoom standup links are posted in the ANNOUNCEMENTS SECTION on your
samagama.in dashboard — look for the announcement bell at the top of the page.
Check it daily before the session.

We do NOT send separate emails for daily standups.

Attending daily standups is MANDATORY for all interns. Missing standups is
treated as missing work.

ABOUT THE KICKOFF ORIENTATION: Session recordings are not shared with interns
who join late (see section 2.6). If you joined late, you are expected to
complete the orientation through a special proctored catch-up path on ViBe,
which includes quizzes. This catch-up is mandatory for late starters before
participating in regular standups.

MODULE 5 | WORK, MENTORSHIP, AND PROJECTS
================================================================================

--- 5.1 | What will I work on? ---
A real open-source project from Vicharanashala's portfolio — assigned based on
your background and the lab's current needs. Areas include: AI/ML, web
development, NLP, computer vision, agriculture-tech (Annam.AI), education-tech
(ViBe), and open-source infrastructure.

We do not pre-publish the exact problem; you choose to join knowing the lab will
assign the project.

--- 5.2 | How many hours per day? ---
Plan for 6 to 10 hours a day, sometimes more during the build phase. This is a
full-time internship for the two-month window. Most candidates who drop out are
juggling something else — VINS expects your FULL ATTENTION.

--- 5.3 | Who is my mentor? ---
You will work with the lab's research and engineering team. The exact mentor
depends on the project. The model is fluid — you will get help from a senior
researcher one day, a peer the next, and someone else for a different question.
That is how real open-source work happens.

--- 5.4 | Is there a stipend? ---
No. The internship is UNPAID. Stellar performers may be recognised with a
discretionary stipend at the lab's option, but this is not promised or expected.

--- 5.5 | Do I need my own laptop? Should I preload any software? ---
Yes — a personal laptop is required. We prefer Linux or macOS. If you use
Windows, please install a terminal that can SSH and run Unix-style commands (WSL
is a clean choice; PuTTY also works). You will be SSH-ing into machines and
using the command line as part of the work.

We do not maintain a fixed software-preload list — your mentor will guide you
on specific tools once your project is assigned.

--- 5.6 | I am using a different email on GitHub / Zoom / the learning platform. Is that okay? ---
No. Your REGISTERED EMAIL is your sole identifier across all programme
platforms. Progress tracking, mentor assignment, and certificate issuance are
all tied to it. Mismatches between platforms CANNOT be retroactively corrected
— use your registered email everywhere from day one.

--- 5.7 | Why has my mentor not been assigned yet or contacted me on Day 1? ---
Mentors are NOT assigned on Day 1. You will be assigned a mentor when you move
to the project phase of VINS, which comes AFTER completing the mandatory
coursework of the Bronze phase (see section 1.3). Once coursework is complete
and you are placed on a project, your mentor will reach out.

NOTE: We do NOT run a Discord server. Communication is through Yaksha chat on
samagama.in and email from no-reply@vicharanashala.ai only (see section 6.1).

--- 8.1 | Does Vicharanashala send a grade report or evaluation to my university? ---
Vicharanashala does NOT send formal evaluation or grade reports to universities
— that process is between you and your college. The certificate issued upon

MODULE 9 | ROSETTA — YOUR INTERNSHIP JOURNAL
================================================================================

--- 9.1 | What is Rosetta? ---
Rosetta is your internship journal — a 65-day document, one entry per day,
every day, for the full duration of Summership 2026. You write in it daily,
keep it privately, and submit it at the end as one of your completion
requirements.

The name comes from the Rosetta Stone — discovered in 1799, it carried the
same text in three scripts and became the key to decoding an ancient language
silent for centuries. Not because it was grand, but because it was honest and
consistent. Sixty-five days of honest writing will become the key to
understanding your own experience.

--- 9.2 | Why does this exist? Is it just busywork? ---
No. It exists for two reasons:

  FOR YOU: Most people go through an intense experience and carry it without
  processing it. They finish and cannot articulate what they actually learned.
  The journal builds that articulation, one day at a time. Students who reflect
  regularly consistently get more out of the programme — not because they work
  harder, but because they understand what they are doing and why.

  FOR US: When you submit Rosetta at the end, it gives us qualitative insight
  into your experience that no survey or evaluation can capture. We use it to
  improve the programme for the next cohort. Your honest voice matters.

--- 9.3 | What is a "thinking routine"? ---
Each day in Rosetta has a thinking routine — a short framework that gives your
reflection a specific shape. Examples:

  3-2-1: 3 things you engaged with, 2 questions on your mind, 1 surprise.
  Muddy / Clear: what is sharp, and what is still foggy.
  What? So What? Now What?: separate facts from meaning from action.

The routines rotate across 65 days so the journal does not feel repetitive or
mechanical. You do not need to research the routine or prepare for it.

--- 9.4 | How do I get my Rosetta journal? ---
Your journal will be shared with you as a Google Doc template link during
orientation. Steps:
  1. Open the link.
  2. Make a copy to your own Google Drive.
  3. Rename it: "Rosetta — [Your Name] — Summership 2026"
  4. That copy is yours for the full 65 days.

DO NOT write in the shared template. Always work in your own copy.

--- 9.5 | How do I use it day to day? ---
  1. Open your Rosetta Google Doc.
  2. Scroll to the entry for today's day number.
  3. Fill in the date at the top of the entry.
  4. Read the thinking routine name and its one-line description.
  5. Answer the three prompts in the writing boxes below.
  6. Close it and get on with your day.

It should take between 10 and 20 minutes. It is not an essay. It is not a
report. It is a journal.

--- 9.6 | How long should each entry be? ---
There is no minimum or maximum word count. A good entry is honest and specific.
Three to five sentences per prompt is usually enough.

WHAT IS NOT ACCEPTABLE: one-word answers, copy-pasted text, vague non-answers
like "today was productive," or anything that reads like it was generated by AI.

--- 9.7 | What is the one rule? ---
WRITE WHAT IS TRUE.

Not what sounds impressive. Not what you think we want to read. Not a polished
summary of the day. If you hated today, write that. If you are confused and
frustrated, write that.

We will know immediately if an entry reads like an LLM wrote it. Do not do
that. The journal only counts as a completion requirement if it is genuinely
yours — in your voice, from your actual experience.

--- 9.8 | Can I use ChatGPT or any AI tool to write my entries? ---
NO. This is the ONE FIRM RULE of Rosetta.

The journal is a record of YOUR thinking, not a demonstration of what an AI
can produce. Entries that read as AI-generated will NOT be counted toward your
completion requirement. If your entire journal reads this way, the journal will
not be accepted.

Use AI tools for your technical work if that is permitted in the programme. Do
NOT use them here.

--- 9.9 | What if I miss a day? ---
Fill it in as soon as you can. Write the ACTUAL DATE you are filling it in, NOT
the date of the missed entry. Be honest in the entry about the fact that you are
writing it late and why.

Do NOT leave entries blank. A late honest entry is always better than no entry.

If you find yourself consistently missing entries, that usually means something
else is going wrong. Use Yaksha in chat, or reach out to your scholar.

--- 9.10 | Will anyone read my journal during the internship? ---
No. We will NOT access your journal during the 65 days. You write it, you keep
it, it is yours.

The only time we read it is AFTER you submit it at the end. This is intentional
— we want you to write freely, without feeling observed.

--- 9.11 | Can the prompts change mid-internship? ---
Occasionally we may update a prompt for a specific day based on what is
happening in the cohort. When this happens, we will announce it through the
programme communication channel BEFORE that day begins.

Check the announcement channel before filling any entry where a change has been
announced. If no announcement has been made, use the prompt as written in your
document.

--- 9.12 | How do I submit Rosetta at the end? ---
On or before Day 65, share your Rosetta Google Doc with the programme
coordinator's email address (shared during wrap-up week). Set the sharing
permission to VIEWER.

CHECKLIST BEFORE SUBMITTING:
  - Your name is in the document title: "Rosetta — [Your Name] — Summership 2026"
  - All 65 entries have been filled in.
  - Your cover page has your name, product, and team filled in.

Rosetta submission is one of the required criteria for receiving your internship
certificate. An incomplete or AI-generated journal will NOT be accepted.

--- 9.13 | I have a question about Rosetta not answered here. What do I do? ---
Ask Yaksha first. If Yaksha cannot answer it, escalate to your programme
coordinator. Do not sit on a question — the journal works best when you start
it right.

--- 9.14 | My college wants written confirmation this is self-paced and won't clash with classes. ---
This is NOT a self-paced internship — it is a very rigorous, time-demanding
programme. It is NOT permitted for one to be part of any other activity during

  5. If the banner still appears after refreshing and you are sure all earlier

--- 12.15 | Why does ViBe sometimes make me re-watch a clip after a quiz? ---
If your answer to the check-in quiz didn't go through correctly, ViBe will take

it is part of the design — NOT a penalty.

Key points:
  - Re-watches are NOT recorded against you. They do not affect your HP or
    evaluation.
  - The clips are short, so a re-watch usually costs less time than guessing
    through multiple retries.
  - Think of the re-watch as the platform helping the idea stick before you
    move on.

--- 12.16 | What kinds of quiz questions will I see on ViBe? ---
ViBe quizzes come in four formats:

  PICK ONE (MCQ): One right answer out of four or so options.
    Tip: Read all options before clicking.

  PICK ONE OR MORE (MSQ): "Select all that apply" — could be one or several.
    Tip: Read each option carefully; small mistakes happen here most.

  TYPE A NUMBER (NAT): A text box asking for a numeric value.
    Tip: Type just the number — no units or extra symbols unless the question
    explicitly asks.

  TRUE OR FALSE: A statement with only two options.
    Tip: A single word like "always," "never," or "only" can flip the correct
    answer.

General tip: Watch the clip first, then answer. Trying to read the question
while the clip is still playing splits your attention.

--- 12.17 | Are the same proctoring rules applied to every course on ViBe? ---
NO — this is one of the most important things to understand.

ViBe's proctoring system is MODULAR. Each individual check — face visibility,

camera/microphone access — can be independently switched on or off by the
instructor for a given course or cohort.

This means:
  - The instructor decides which proctoring elements are active for their course.
  - Some courses run with all checks active (typical for internships and
    credentialed programmes).
  - Other courses may run with only a subset active.
  - Certain pilot or open courses may have most proctoring relaxed.

IMPORTANT: Do NOT assume rules carry across courses. A relaxation given in one
course does NOT transfer to another. Always check the course-specific guidelines
shared by your instructor before each course you join.

When in doubt, ask your instructor or programme coordinator which proctoring
elements are active for your current course.

--- 12.18 | What does the "quiet helper" on ViBe actually do? ---
While a lesson plays, a small helper runs gently on your device using your
camera and microphone. It checks, in real time, that basic conditions for
learning are present. It looks at five things:

  1. A face is visible — to confirm you are really there.
  2. Only one face is in frame — to confirm the learning is yours.
  3. There is enough light on your face — a silhouette is hard to recognise.
  4. The room isn't full of voices — no talking, TV, or background podcasts.
  5. You are looking at the screen — brief glances away are fine; long stretches
     feel like drifting off.

The helper is NOT a judge. Brief, normal movements (a stretch, a sneeze, a
glance at your notebook) are absolutely fine. It only pays attention to
sustained patterns, not split-second things.

--- 12.19 | Does ViBe record long videos of me while I'm learning? ---
No. ViBe does NOT continuously record videos of you.

  - Camera and microphone are used for REAL-TIME PRESENCE CHECKS ONLY.
  - Long recordings of your face or voice are NOT stored.
  - When the lesson ends, the helper goes quiet too.
  - All data is handled strictly in accordance with the consent terms shown when
    you signed up.

Think of the helper as a quiet study partner sitting beside you — there if
needed, invisible otherwise.

--- 12.20 | What is the single most common avoidable mistake learners make? ---
Sitting with a WINDOW DIRECTLY BEHIND YOU during the day.

The room may feel bright to you, but your camera only sees a dark silhouette
where your face should be. The helper then struggles to confirm your presence,
and your lesson may pause or rewind.

THE FIX: Move so the window is to your side or in front of the laptop, not
behind you. A lamp placed in front of you works just as well in the evening.

--- 12.21 | Why does the lesson keep pausing or restarting even when I'm paying attention? ---
The cause is almost always something small in your environment, not the platform
itself. Run through this checklist:

  - Your face is too dark → add a lamp in front of you.
  - Your face is partly out of frame → raise the laptop or sit a bit closer.
  - There are voices in the background → close the door, or move to a quieter room.
  - You switched tabs or went idle → stay on the ViBe tab; take breaks BETWEEN
    clips, not during them.
  - Camera or mic permission dropped → check the lock icon in your browser's
    address bar and confirm both are set to "Allow".

--- 12.22 | Can I read the quiz questions aloud or mutter to myself while watching? ---
It's best not to. The microphone listens for sustained voices in the room, and
reading aloud, muttering, or asking a roommate "wait, what was that?" can all
be picked up as anomalies.

Simple habit: watch in silence, answer in silence, and chat freely during breaks
between clips.

--- 12.23 | Can I study with a friend on camera since we're learning together? ---
In most courses, NO. Only you — the registered learner — should be in the
camera frame during a lesson. The helper checks that exactly ONE FACE is visible
at a time.

Group study is a wonderful habit — just not inside a ViBe session itself. A
good way to do it:
  - Hop on a separate call with your friend on your phone or a second device.
  - Discuss concepts BEFORE or AFTER a ViBe lesson, not during it.
  - Return to ViBe alone when you're ready to watch the clip and attempt the quiz.

NOTE: As explained in section 12.17, the single-face-in-frame check is an
instructor-controlled setting. A few courses (typically paired-learning pilots)
may explicitly relax this rule. Do NOT assume relaxation in one course applies
to another — always default to studying alone unless your instructor has clearly
stated otherwise for that specific course.

--- 12.24 | Will I lose my progress if I clear my browser or reinstall it? ---
No. Your progress is saved ON THE SERVER, tied to your registered email — NOT
on your browser or computer.

Refreshing the page, clearing cache, switching browsers, or reinstalling your
browser will NOT wipe your progress. The moment you log back in with the same
registered email, all your completed clips and quizzes will be exactly where
you left them.

When in doubt: log out, log back in.

--- 12.25 | Is there a recommended daily learning rhythm on ViBe? ---
Yes — small, regular sessions work far better than long marathon sessions. A
practical rhythm:
  - Show up daily, even if only for thirty minutes. Daily consistency beats a
    five-hour weekend cram.
  - Take breaks between clips, not during them. A clip is short — finish it first.
  - Treat each quiz as a gentle check-in, not a high-stakes test.
  - For programmes with deadlines (such as internships), aim for the daily
    progress target announced by your programme team — typically around 3.33%
    per day.

NOTE: The pacing above is a general guideline only. For any specific programme,
the actual milestones, deadlines, and weekly progress targets will be announced
by your instructors or programme team. That takes precedence over the general
guidance here.

--- 12.26 | What should my "study corner" look like before I start a ViBe session? ---
A ViBe-friendly study spot needs only three things:
  1. LIGHT IN FRONT OF YOUR FACE — a lamp or window facing you, never behind.
  2. JUST YOU IN THE CAMERA FRAME — ask family, friends, or pets not to wander
     through.
  3. A REASONABLY QUIET ROOM — no TV, no music with words, no one else on a
     call nearby.

Two minutes of setup before you start saves a lot of small frustrations during
the lesson.

--- 13.16 | We selected Project X as our top priority but were assigned Project Y. Can we change it? ---
No. Project assignments are FINAL and cannot be changed. Allocation is done to
ensure balanced distribution across projects.

--- 13.17 | I just started the internship. Can I form my own team now? ---
No. For later cohorts, teams will be randomly assigned. Please wait for the
official communication.

--- 13.18 | When do team activities begin? ---
Team-based work begins in PHASE 2. During Phase 1 (online coursework), you do
not need to worry about team activities.

--- 13.19 | Can I request a specific teammate after teams are assigned? ---
No. Team assignments are FINAL and requests for changes are not entertained.

--- 13.20 | What happens if a team member is inactive or not contributing? ---
Report the issue to your mentor/scholar early. Prolonged inactivity may lead to
administrative intervention.

--- 13.21 | Can I switch teams if there are conflicts? ---
Team switches are NOT ALLOWED except in exceptional, admin-approved cases
involving serious concerns.

--- 13.22 | Will team performance affect individual evaluation? ---
Yes. While some components may be individual, team deliverables are a key part
of evaluation.

--- 13.23 | How will communication happen within teams? ---
Teams are expected to self-organise internal team coordination (e.g., a small
WhatsApp/email thread limited to the four teammates). Official programme updates
will continue to come via email and Yaksha chat on samagama.in.

NOTE: This in-team coordination is DISTINCT from the unofficial cohort-wide
channels prohibited under section 6.1 — a four-person team thread for project
work is fine; any group that purports to act on behalf of Vicharanashala or the
wider cohort is NOT.

--- 13.24 | What if I miss the team allocation email? ---
All communication is sent via registered email. You are responsible for
checking regularly, INCLUDING SPAM FOLDERS.

--- 13.25 | Can a team be dissolved and reformed? ---
No. Once finalised, teams are LOCKED and cannot be dissolved.

--- 13.26 | What happens if I drop out of the internship? ---
Your team will be adjusted accordingly, and the remaining members may continue
as a team of three or receive a replacement.

--- 13.27 | Will we get time to know our teammates before Phase 2? ---
Yes. There is typically a buffer period before Phase 2 where teams can connect
and prepare.
